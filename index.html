3. Location Data

Orbit does not use your device's GPS, location services, or real-time position in any way. We never request location permission. We do not track where you are or have been.

The only location data in Orbit is what you choose to type yourself — a home city or place name such as "London" or "Cape Town." This is a general place, not a precise address or exact coordinate.

When you enter a place name, the app looks up approximate city-level coordinates from a built-in reference list. These coordinates are used solely to:

  • Display your home as a pin on the Orbit globe
  • Calculate the rough distance between your home and your connections' homes

These coordinates come from the place name you typed — not from your device's GPS. You can update or remove your home location at any time from your Account settings.

4. Find Friends & Contacts

The Find Friends feature helps you discover which of your existing contacts are already on Orbit. Using it is entirely optional. Declining contacts permission has no effect on any other part of the app.

How it works, technically

When you tap Find Friends, Orbit asks your device's operating system for access to your contacts. Before anything is sent over the network, each phone number in your contacts is converted on your device into a SHA-256 hash — a one-way cryptographic fingerprint. Think of it as a unique code derived from the number that cannot be reversed back into the original digits.

Raw phone numbers from your contacts never leave your phone. Our servers only ever receive these irreversible hashes. This means that even in the unlikely event of a server breach, the phone numbers of people in your contacts who are not Orbit users cannot be recovered from our systems — because they were never there.

What we do and don't store

  • We do not store your contacts list
  • We do not retain the hashes of non-Orbit-user contacts after the query completes
  • We do not use contact data for any purpose other than the single matching query you initiate
  • We do store a SHA-256 hash of your own phone number in your profile, so that others can find you via Find Friends

This approach is the same method used by privacy-focused messaging applications to implement contact discovery without exposing users' address books.

9. Security

We apply the following technical measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption in transit: all communication between the app and our servers uses HTTPS/TLS
  • Authentication security: managed by Supabase Auth using industry-standard bcrypt password hashing
  • Row Level Security (RLS): database policies ensure that users can only read or modify their own data — not other users' records
  • Phone number hashing: phone numbers used for Find Friends matching are stored only as SHA-256 hashes, not as raw numbers
  • No GPS collection: Orbit does not request or access device location services at any point

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a breach that may affect your data, we will notify affected users promptly.

10. Data Retention

We keep your personal data for as long as your account is active. When you delete your account, all associated data — including your name, email, phone number, phone hash, home location, and connection records — is permanently and irreversibly deleted from our database within 30 days.

You can delete your account at any time from the Account tab inside the app. No email, no waiting period, no dark patterns.
